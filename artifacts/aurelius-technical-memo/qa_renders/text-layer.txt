A R C H I T E C T U R E , P U B L I C - R E P L A Y E V I D E N C E , A N D A H I S T O R I C A L V A L I D A T I O N P R O P O S A L
Aurelius does not replace Kubernetes, Slurm, serving schedulers, fleet-management systems, or
existing control planes. It sits above them as a predictive supervisory layer.
Existing systems make sophisticated decisions at each layer. Aurelius tests whether those decisions can be
improved by coordinating them against a shared prediction of future fleet state, simulating coupled alternatives
before execution, and returning the best safe action through the existing control plane.
T H E P R O B L E M
At fleet scale, individually reasonable decisions about placement, capacity, routing, batching, prewarming, admission,
migration, and serving policy interact. Their downstream effects arrive on different time horizons and cannot be evaluated
reliably as isolated knobs. Some of the constraints that decide a decision's economics emerge only after it has executed.

Each control period, Aurelius advances a persistent forward model of the fleet, generates a bounded set of high-value
coupled candidate actions, evaluates each candidate on a read-only simulated future, rejects anything that fails a hard SLA,
capacity, power, policy, or safety gate, and returns the best feasible action through the systems that remain authoritative.

plans against a prediction of where that state is going, and returns recommendations to the systems that already

03 · WHY THIS IS HARD AT SCALE
T H E C O U P L I N G P R O B L E M
A fleet does not face one scheduling problem
A large fleet faces thousands of interacting decisions made at different layers and over different horizons. A
locally correct action can change the value or feasibility of a later action elsewhere in the system. The effects also
land on different clocks: batching within the current window, capacity and prewarming over subsequent periods,
placement and migration well beyond them.

T H E V A L U E H Y P O T H E S I S
The hypothesis is not that existing controllers are unsophisticated. It is that the best action at one layer may
depend on future conditions and decisions at several other layers.
W H E R E C U R R E N T - S T A T E O P T I M I Z A T I O N S T A R T S T O O L A T E
Some important costs and constraints emerge only after a placement or capacity decision has already been made:
capacity and power headroom, congestion and topology pressure, demand, and the wholesale price of electricity,
which on public day-ahead markets can swing several-fold within a day. Once the decision lands, those costs are
largely locked in. A forward model of future fleet state allows those conditions to influence the decision before
execution rather than explain it afterward. No single lever is appropriate for every workload: candidates are
generated per operating regime and workload class, and hard policy gates reject any bundle that is out of bounds
for the work it would touch, before ranking.

04 · HOW AURELIUS WORKS
T H E C O N T R O L L O O P
Forecast, simulate, constrain, decide
One loop, six stages, each control period. The loop is deliberately conservative: prediction and search are allowed
to be ambitious because nothing they produce reaches execution without passing the gates.
01 PERSISTENT FORWARD MODEL
A stateful model advances the fleet through time rather than treating
the system as a snapshot of free GPUs. It carries server and rack
capacity, placement and locality, queue dynamics, replica warm and
cold state, migrations in flight, and the operator cost basis, including
warm-hold and migration penalties. Inputs are tagged by
provenance, so a modeled value is never treated as a measured one.
02 CAUSAL FORECASTS
Forecasts of arrival rate, request characteristics, queue dynamics,
and infrastructure and pricing conditions, including wholesale
electricity price, use only information available at the current decision
point. No future arrivals and no future prices leak into any decision, in
planning or in evaluation.
03 PHYSICS-GUIDED CANDIDATE GENERATION
A bounded set of plausible, high-value coupled bundles is generated
from the operating regime: timing, placement, routing, capacity,
batching, prewarming, admission, migration, precision, and clock
policy are proposed together rather than tuned as independent
knobs. Known-strong candidates are always retained, so a valuable
option is not silently excluded.
04 READ-ONLY COUNTERFACTUAL SIMULATION
Each candidate is evaluated on an isolated simulated future
advanced by the forward model. Candidate evaluation never mutates
the live system, and scoring is repeatable and auditable after the fact.
05 OBJECTIVE AND HARD GATES
The objective is SLA-safe goodput per operator dollar: SLA-meeting
requests over infrastructure cost, with energy at the prevailing market
price plus warm-hold and migration cost, and a forecast-uncertainty
penalty in the same economic terms. Hard capacity, SLA, power,
policy, residency, and safety constraints reject infeasible candidates
before ranking; a Pareto gate accepts a recommendation only if it
beats the baseline without regressing SLA.
06 RECOMMENDATION AND FALLBACK
The best feasible candidate is returned as a recommendation
through the existing control plane. When forecast confidence is
insufficient, Aurelius falls back deterministically to the configured
safe default rather than acting on a low-confidence forecast.
The optimizer cannot select a decision it never evaluates.
Which candidates enter the search is a first-class
engineering problem, measured under a regret audit, not
an afterthought.
W H Y C A N D I D A T E G E N E R A T I O N I S A F I R S T - C L A S S P R O B L E M
The sharpest finding in the current benchmark cycle was not about forecasting. With the forward model, simulator, objective, and safety
gates all unchanged, enabling more decision surfaces initially made results worse. The cause was containment: the candidate set had
collapsed around a single surface, so the bundle that actually wins an expensive electricity window (lower precision, aggressive batching,
and a higher clock) was never in the set being evaluated. Restoring the physics-guided candidate set recovered most of the opportunity;
letting the coupled search reach combinations a fixed grid does not contain recovered the rest. Where exhaustive enumeration was
tractable, the bounded search matched it exactly, at roughly 40 evaluations per decision. The residual gap to a forecast oracle, about 4.5
percent, is forecast quality, not search.

05 · PUBLIC-REPLAY EVIDENCE
T H E R E S U L T
A large signal, with its scope attached
Replaying the full selected public-trace windows with the per-period request cap removed produces the largest
verified result in the program. It is reported the way it should be read: baseline defined, load disclosed, scope
attached.
The comparison policy is a constructed production-class composite: one deterministic, causal policy assembled from
established production serving techniques. Continuous, iteration-level batching [1,2]; SLA-aware ordering with deadline
protection and class-based admission under pressure [3]; KV and prefix-cache-aware routing [4];
topology-aware placement [5]; and backlog-driven autoscaling with warm-pool headroom [6,7]. It sees no future arrivals and no
future prices. It is a public-replay composite, not any specific operator's internal scheduler.

06 · PROPOSED NEXT STEP
T H E P R O P O S A L
A bounded, read-only historical evaluation
The next question is not whether the public benchmark reproduces exactly on another fleet. It is whether
predictive cross-layer planning identifies meaningful, causally defensible counterfactual improvements on real
historical fleet behavior.

cluster, or bounded historical

The precise schema would be minimized to the selected evaluation. Nothing outside it is requested.
N O T R E Q U I R E D
Historical replay precedes any shadow-mode
recommendation, and shadow mode precedes
any optional production integration. Every stage
remains under operator control.
E V A L U A T I O N O U T P U T S

Identify one technically appropriate owner and one bounded historical dataset on which the predictive-
planning hypothesis can be evaluated.
Public replay establishes a testable signal. Historical operator telemetry is the next step required to determine
whether the architecture generalizes.
